Help

Access the System

To request access to the system, send your email address to christopher.greengrass@uhl-tr.nhs.uk or ring 0116 204 4771. Chris will register your email address in the system, that will allow you to set a password.

Setting Your Password

Once your email address has been registered, you can use the First Login or Forgotten Password link on the login page to set your password.

After clicking the button you will be shown the Send Password Reset screen.

Enter the email address that was registered on the site into the text box and click the Send Set Password Link button.

A few moments later you should receive an email from the portal containing a long link. Click on the link or paste it into a browser address bar to be taken to the Password Reset screen. Be warned that the link will expire after a few hours, but you can always request another email to be sent.

You can now enter the password that you want to use to log into the GENVASC Portal.

Recruits List

The recruits listing page shows all participants recruited at your practice, regardless of whether the details were entered at the practice or at the LCBRU.

The participants are shown in with the most recently recruited participant at the top in pages of 10 participants per page. If more than one page of participants is available, a page navigator will be displayed at the bottom of the page that will allow you to move to other pages.

Recruits Details

Recruit details are displayed in a series of panels, with the participants name across the top, followed by 2 columns of details.

When a recruit is first entered, their name will be blank. The name will be filled in when the details for the participant have been confirmed by staff at the LCBRU.

The left panel shows the details submitted for the participant by the practice. This includes that participant's NHS Number, date of birth and the date of recruitment. If these details were entered by the GP practice, the name of the person who entered the details will also be shown here.

The right panel show the details of the participant's registration at the LCBRU. This include's the status of the enrolment, the participant's study identifier, the date that the participant was processed by the LCBRU and the member of staff that carried out the processing. Until the details have been processed by the LCBRU, all of these details except for the status will be blank.

The status field can have one of a few values. An explanation of these status is listed below:

Created
The details have been created on the portal, but have not yet been transferred to the LCBRU. The recruitment usually only stays in this status for less than an hour.
Awaiting processing
Details of the participant have been transferred to the LCBRU and are awaiting processing. Once the participant's demographoics have been obtained from NHS Digital and the blood sample has arrived at the LCBRU, the participant will be processed. This could take several days.
Recruited
The demographics and blood sample have arrived at the LCBRU and the recruit has been successfully processed.
Available for Cohort
The participant has been enrolled into the GENVASC study for more than 30 days and the participants details are now available to the LCBRU staff for research.
Excluded
The recruitment was excluded from the GENVASC study for some reason. This may have been that the participant was found not to have met the inclusion criteria, have met an exclusion criterion, or was already enrolled in the GENVASC study.
Withdraw
The recruit was successfully recruited into the GENVASC study, but at a later date decided to withdraw from the study.

Recruits Searching

At the top of the recruit listing in a box that allows you to search for participants by name, NHS Number of study identifier. You can search for either the full name or identifier or just a part of it. For example, if you type in the phrase 'mar'' and press enter or click the Search button, all participants that have 'mar' in their name, such as Mary, Marie,Mark or Marcus, will be displayed.

To clear the search criterion and go back to a full list of participants, click the Clear Search button.

Entering a new Recruit

To enter the details of a new recruit, click the Add Recruit button on the recruit listing.

Each new recruit requires 3 pieces of information:

NHS Number
Must be a valid NHS Number.
Date of Birth
Age of the participants should be between 40 and 70 years old.
Date Recruited
Cannot be a date in the furture.

Once the details have been entered, click the Save button to submit the recruit details.

Editing Recruits

The details of a recruit can be edited up to the point that the recruit is processed by the LCBRU. To edit the participant details, click the Edit button in the recruit panel.
